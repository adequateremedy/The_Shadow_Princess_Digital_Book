The pain subsided - not completely, and not indefinitely.
Only enough for Morrigan to breathe.
Enough for her to think, for her to understand.
She sat upon the edge of the narrow cot, gripping Dagda's hand so tightly her knuckles 
turned pale - another contraction rolled through her.
This one was deep, powerful, and ancient.
Unlike anything she had ever felt before.
The sensation stole her native tongue, all her certainty, and it left Morrigan only with
instinct. Dagda remained beside her.
One hand supporting her shoulders.
The other trapped firmly within her grasp.
He neither noticed nor cared.
The shadows crowded every corner of the small tent.
And though they were silent, they were attentive, and just as concerned as Dagda.
The sight would have frightened anyone unfamiliar with them.
They were protective.
They were family.
Morrigan lowered her head.
She closed her eyes, and took a deep breath.
Her daughter shifted again.
This time – a strong movement that felt predetermined.
Morrigan had never felt more drained, in her life.
Then tears gathered unexpectedly.
Not from pain – but from emotion, wonder, fear… and from love.
Everything together, all at once.
She screamed, her voice hitting a shrieking pitch that echoed in a way even the trees 
and shadows could not keep hidden.
Dagda brushed damp hair from her face.
His touch remained steady and gentle, and still, she could feel the tension beneath it.
The fear.
He hid it well.
He always had.
Not from weakness, but necessity.
Because someone needed to remain calm.
Because others depended upon him.
Tonight she saw through it easily.
Tonight he was simply a father terrified for the ones he loved most.
________________________________________
The tent gradually filled with others who had been concerned about the screaming.
Word traveled far too quickly.
Several fae healers arrived first.
Then members of Dagda and Morrigan’s Faction.
Then others - concerned friends, refugees, and a few strangers even.
The tent soon overflowed into the area directly outside it.
Not with noise, but with anticipation.
The atmosphere felt strange, like it was being charged.
Similar to the moments before lightning strikes the ground next to you.
Everyone felt it, but no one understood it.
Morrigan’s labor itself was not unusual.
Pregnant Fae gave birth every day across Midgard.
Fae children entered the world constantly, yet… something about this felt different.
And though they could not speak, the shadows knew it.
The forest knew it too.
Even the air seemed to know.
Outside, Crudsder remained awake.
Lanterns flickered throughout the city.
People watched.
Waited.
Listened.
Not because they expected miracles.
But because the screams from the forest were getting louder and more intense.
And this was surely going to attract unwanted attention.
________________________________________
By dawn the contractions had strengthened considerably.
Morrigan's world narrowed.
Pain, breathing, movement, rest… then pain again.
Time had lost meaning to her as minutes stretched endlessly, and hours vanished 
unexpectedly. The tent blurred around her.
Faces came and went.
Voices faded into background noise.
Only certain things remained clear; permanent things:
Dagda, their daughter, the shadows.
Everyone and everything else became distant - secondary.
At one point she opened her eyes and found Dagda staring at her.
Not speaking.
Simply watching.
The expression on his face nearly shattered her composure.
Love, helplessness, fear, pride.
Every emotion was visible at once.
He looked as though he wanted to carry the pain himself.
As though he would if given the opportunity.
Morrigan managed a weak smile.
Immediately his eyes softened.
The reaction felt automatic - instinctive.
The way breathing was instinctive.
The way loving him had become instinctive.
Years ago, she would never have imagined this life they built.
Never imagined a child – let alone a daughter.
Never imagined this… moment.
The realization struck unexpectedly.
They had survived so much together.
And now they stood here.
At the edge of something entirely new.
________________________________________
Beyond the city, the woodlands continued protecting.
Few noticed at first.
The trees stood where they always stood.
The paths remained unchanged.
The streams still flowed.
Yet something subtle had shifted.
Animals had begun gathering around their camp, deep in the woodlands that bordered the 
west of Crudsder. Meanwhile the birds began filling branches, until no more fit. Then they 
began gathering unusually close to the city.  Perching themselves wherever they could.
Meanwhile, bears, foxes, and even wolves entered the city without fear; walking toward the 
forested western edge, past the city itself, as if they already held devotion in their souls, 
and were being called. 
Deer stood by the edge of the woodlands, watching the mortals who were 
listening and whispering. The natural world appeared drawn toward something inside the 
forest that bordered the city. Toward something or someone - not yet born.
Word spread quietly to the Fae in Crudsder, and eventually flowed into mortal ears and 
subsequently from mortal lips, with no filter.  
Soon, all in Crudsder knew about the camp of Fae in the woodlands - the forest 
itself was responding. No one knew how, nor did they know why; only that it was 
happening. Even Oberon was noted among them, exchanging troubled glances.
Because some things existed beyond explanation, beyond understanding, and tradition.
And for Oberon and Mab, this felt like one of those things.
________________________________________
By midday the first scouts reached Crudsder.
Not many, yet their arrival changed everything.
Messengers arrived breathless and terrified.
Urgent with reports.
Human patrols were entering the eastern roads.
More followed behind them.
The pursuit continued.
The hunters had not stopped.
They had not given up.
The news of animals gathering near the woodlands of Crudsder had spread rapidly.
Panic threatened daily life for all.
The city teetered upon the edge of chaos, and Dagda had received the report personally.
He listened in silence.
His expression never changed.
Yet inside, dread tightened like a vice.
The timing could not have been worse.
Morrigan needed him.
Their daughter needed him.
The Fae needed him.
His Faction needed him.
Every responsibility collided simultaneously.
For a moment he simply stood there – motionless and overwhelmed.
Then Morrigan's voice broke through the noise.
For a brief moment – it was quiet.
She was exhausted, but certain.
"Go!" she pushed through another contraction with gritted teeth.
Dagda looked down at her and immediately shook his head.
"No!"
The answer emerged instantly, without hesitation.
Morrigan almost laughed.
Even now, here – he was still being stubborn.
Hopelessly stubborn.
Another contraction interrupted the thought.
Pain swept through her.
Strong enough to steal breath and bend reality.
When it finally passed, she looked at him again.
"You have to…"
Dagda stared into her eyes.
Morrigan reached for his hand and held it tightly.
"I cannot leave either of you!”
His statement sounded more like a demand.
Yet both understood the weight behind it.
Trust… faith… love.
Dagda closed his eyes briefly.
Every possible choice hurt.
Finally, Morrigan nodded.
Once, reluctantly. Then Dagda pressed his forehead against hers.
It was a silent promise, or perhaps a prayer.
Yet painful all the same.
________________________________________
The eastern defenses formed quickly.
There were no walls protecting Crudsder.
Only obedient forests and fearful Fae.
Only allied mortals and devoted animals.
They had not gathered to protect Morrigan, Dagda, or their unborn child, but to protect all 754 Fae from the camp.
Fae from both Courts: Seelie and UnSeelie, as well as every Faction present, united.
Politics no longer mattered, as they were all in danger now.
Survival was all that mattered now.
The woods themselves became allies.
Paths concealed movement.
Trees provided cover.
Shadows carried information.
The hunters found progress difficult, confusing, and extremely frustrating.
The woodlands resisted them.
Actively and deliberately.
Branches blocked roads - intentionally.
Trails that were previously marked - vanished unexpectedly.
Directions became unreliable.
The deeper the hunters advanced, the stranger everything felt.
Dagda sensed it immediately.
The woods were responding.
Not merely existing.
Responding.
To the shadows… the unknown… the liminal space being brought forth.
The realization settled heavily inside him.
Because it should not have been possible.
Yet the evidence surrounded him, everywhere.
His child had not even been born yet, and still the world reacted.
________________________________________
Hour after hour.
The pain intensified.
The healers worked tirelessly.
Comfort, guidance, support.
Nothing more.
Nature demanded rest.
Morrigan drifted between exhaustion and determination.
Between agony and anticipation.
Between fear and joy.
The boundaries blurred, time blurred, even reality blurred.
At one point she thought she heard singing.
Soft voices drifting through the tents of their camp.
Ancient melodies.
Gentle melodies that moved with the shadows.
Perhaps they were real.
Perhaps they were just imagined.
The distinction no longer mattered.
The sound comforted her and wrapped around her like a warm bathing spring.
Her daughter moved constantly now.
As if impatient, almost eager.
The sensation felt impossible to explain.
It was as though the child inside her knew something.
As though she could already feel the doorway waiting ahead.
________________________________________
Sunset arrived beneath crimson skies.
Morrigan’s labor had entered its final stage.
Every Shadow Fae could feel it too.
The healers sensed it, some fae even awaited it, though not all were even concerned with it, 
as their lives were in danger.
But… Dagda had longed for it.
And it felt as if the entire city seemed to somehow know it too.
Outside the tent, refugees gathered quietly.
Watching the forest, listening, and waiting.
Not because they expected salvation, but because they needed something beautiful to 
happen. Something hopeful and promising.
The world had offered too much death, fear, and loss recently.
Tonight, the fae that gathered around their tent needed life, even if they were not fully aware 
of the ramifications.
Inside the tent, Morrigan cried out.
The sound echoed through the camp, and into the city.
The contraction hit harder than any before – like a force of nature, or tidal wave.
The shadows surged instantly – the tent flickered between light and dark.
Darkness would spill across walls, ceilings, and even across the dirt floors.
Not threatening – but none the less – protective and reactive.
The tent would darken, and then brighten, then darkened again.
Reality seemed to ripple for Morrigan
The healers exchanged panicked looks, though none spoke because none of them fully 
Understood. 
Outside, the forest shuddered.
Every tree and branch and root.
It was as if a single pulse spread through the woodlands, outside Crudsder.
It felt like Mother Nature herself had inhaled deep and held her breath.
And somewhere deep within the ancient woods west of Crudsder, hidden beyond sight, hidden beyond maps, hidden beyond history itself—
A seam appeared - thin as a thread.
Nearly invisible.
Waiting.
The boundary between Midgard and another realm had begun to weaken.
Not from force.
Not from ritual.
Not from intention.
But, from birth and blood.
From Akasha.
From the child that was coming.
And both realms were already changing to make room for her.
